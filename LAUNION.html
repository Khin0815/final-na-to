The History of La Union

⚓ A Province Born from Union

Founded in 1850, La Union's name comes from its creation through the union of three provinces.

🏛️ Spanish Era

La Union was officially established on March 2, 1850, by the Spanish colonial government. Its name literally translates to "the Union," symbolizing the merger of the southern towns of Ilocos Sur, northern towns of Pangasinan, and western towns of Benguet.

1850

🌊 Surf Culture

San Juan became the surfing capital in the 1980s, hosting international competitions yearly and drawing surfers from around the world to its legendary breaks.

Since 1980s

🎨 Heritage & Arts

Home to the famous "Basi" wine, inabel weaving, and the vibrant "Pindang" Festival — a rich cultural tapestry woven through centuries of tradition.

Cultural Hub

🗣️ Language

True to its rich historical melting pot of Ilocano, Pangasinense, and Cordilleran cultures, La Union was the first province in the Philippines to declare Ilocano a co-official language.
