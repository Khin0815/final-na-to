❓ FAQs

Everything you need to know about visiting La Union.

🚗 Getting to La Union

La Union is approximately 250–280 km north of Manila, which is about 4–6 hours by road depending on traffic. The most common route is via the NLEX–TPLEX expressway heading towards San Fernando City.

Yes! Several bus lines operate from Manila to San Fernando, La Union including Partas, Dominion, and Maria de Leon. Most depart from Cubao or Pasay terminals and the trip takes around 5–6 hours. Bus trips to San Juan are also available.

There is no commercial airport in La Union. The nearest major airports are NAIA in Manila and the Laoag International Airport in Ilocos Norte. Most visitors travel by road from Manila.

🏖️ Beaches & Surfing

The best months for surfing are October to February when the northeast monsoon (amihan) brings consistent swells. For calm swimming, March to May offers warm, clear waters. La Union is generally visitable year-round.

No! San Juan Beach is one of the best places in Asia to learn to surf. Dozens of surf schools and instructors offer beginner lessons daily. Board rentals are also widely available along the beach strip.

Most beaches like San Juan and Bauang are free to access. Some resort beaches require a minimum spend or entrance fee if you're using their facilities. Public sections are always free.

🍜 Food & Dining

The absolute must-tries are Baluarte (Ilocano grilled meat), Pindang (salted dried fish), Inasin (grilled salted fish), and Basi wine. For cafes, El Union Coffee's dirty horchata is practically a pilgrimage destination for coffee lovers.

While traditional Ilocano cuisine is largely meat and seafood-based, many modern cafes and restaurants in San Juan cater to vegetarians and vegans. Look for places like Great Northwest and the various cafes along the beach strip.
